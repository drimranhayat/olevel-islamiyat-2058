Paper 1 Course Plan

This page gives students and parents a clear view of what is covered in O Level Islamiyat Paper 1 and how students are trained for strong exam responses.

Qur’an

Major Themes of the Qur’an

Students study selected Qur’anic passages by understanding their theme, meaning, message, and relevance to Muslim belief and daily life.

  • Allah in Himself
  • Allah’s relationship with the created world
  • Allah’s Messengers
  • Importance of Qur’anic teachings today
Qur’an

History & Importance of the Qur’an

This area covers revelation, preservation, compilation, and the Qur’an’s central role as the primary source of Islamic guidance.

  • First revelation
  • Modes and stages of revelation
  • Compilation under the Caliphs
  • Qur’an as a source of law and guidance

  • Prophet ﷺ as a model for Muslims

Why Paper 1 Needs Special Preparation

Paper 1 contains both factual topics and significance-based questions. Students need strong knowledge, but they also need to explain why events, teachings, and personalities matter.

Content + Significance

Many students know the story of an event, but they lose marks when they cannot explain its importance. This course trains students to connect facts with meaning, impact, and relevance.

Main Topics Covered in Paper 1

Each topic is taught with background, key facts, important terms, common question patterns, and guided answer-writing practice.

Theme 1

Allah in Himself

Students learn how selected Qur’anic passages present Allah’s oneness, power, knowledge, mercy, authority, and uniqueness.

Theme 2

Allah and the Created World

Students study how the Qur’an describes creation, signs of Allah, human responsibility, gratitude, obedience, and accountability.

Theme 3

Allah and His Messengers

Students understand the role of prophets, revelation, guidance, obedience to Allah, and the continuity of divine message.

Qur’an History

Revelation and Compilation

Students learn the process of revelation and how the Qur’an was preserved, collected, and compiled for the Muslim community.

Seerah

Makkan and Madinan Life

Students study the Prophet’s ﷺ mission, preaching, persecution, migration, leadership, treaties, battles, and community building.

Community

First Islamic Community

Students learn about the Prophet’s ﷺ family, wives, descendants, leading Companions, Emigrants, Helpers, and scribes.

Paper 1 Answer-Writing Training

Students are guided to write answers that are clear, relevant, detailed, and connected to the wording of the question.

Step 1

Understand the Question

Students learn how to identify whether the question asks for description, explanation, significance, comparison, or personal response.

Step 2

Build Detailed Paragraphs

Students practise paragraph development with accurate facts, names, events, Islamic terms, and relevant examples.

Step 3

Explain Importance

Students learn to explain why Qur’anic teachings, Seerah events, and early community figures are significant for Muslims.

Common Problem

Students Write Too Generally

In Paper 1, weak answers often give general Islamic information but do not directly answer the specific question. Students are trained to stay focused on the exact wording.

Course Solution

Question-Focused Practice

Students practise topic-wise questions and learn how to select relevant material, avoid unnecessary details, and develop exam-ready answers.
