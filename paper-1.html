O Level Islamiyat Paper 1 Complete Syllabus Outline

A clear, syllabus-based roadmap for Cambridge O Level Islamiyat 2058 Paper 1, covering Qur’anic themes, the history and importance of the Qur’an, the life and importance of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, and the first Islamic community.

Paper 1 at a Glance

  • Written paper: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Total marks: 50
  • Question 1 and Question 2 are compulsory
  • Students answer two further questions
  • Focus on knowledge, explanation, and significance
  • Ideal for topic-wise notes and answer-writing practice

Paper 1 Syllabus Areas

Paper 1 is built around four major syllabus areas. Each area requires accurate knowledge, clear explanation, and the ability to discuss the importance of beliefs, events, personalities, and teachings in Islam.

Section 1

Major Themes of the Qur’an

Close study of selected Qur’anic passages, their themes, meanings, and importance in the lives of Muslims.

Section 2

History & Importance of the Qur’an

Revelation, compilation, preservation, legal significance, and the Qur’an as the basis of Islamic thought and action.

Section 3

Life & Importance of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

Main events of the Prophet’s ﷺ life, his character, leadership, struggles, achievements, and significance for Muslims.

Section 4

The First Islamic Community

The Prophet’s ﷺ family, wives, descendants, Companions, Emigrants, Helpers, scribes, and leading figures.

1. Major Themes of the Qur’an

Students study selected passages from the Qur’an in Arabic and English. The focus is on identifying the main themes, explaining their meaning, and understanding their importance in Muslim belief and life today.

Theme Group 1

God in Himself

This group focuses on Allah’s oneness, uniqueness, power, knowledge, authority, mercy, and absolute independence.

  • Sura 2.255
  • Sura 6.101–103
  • Sura 41.37
  • Sura 42.4–5
  • Sura 112
Theme Group 2

God’s Relationship with the Created World

This group explores Allah as Creator, Sustainer, Lord, Guide, Protector, Judge, and the source of all human dependence.

  • Sura 1
  • Sura 2.21–22
  • Sura 96.1–5
  • Sura 99
  • Sura 114
Theme Group 3

God’s Messengers

This group explains prophethood, revelation, guidance, obedience to Allah, human responsibility, and the role of Messengers.

  • Sura 2.30–37
  • Sura 6.75–79
  • Sura 5.110
  • Sura 93
  • Sura 108

2. The History and Importance of the Qur’an

This section develops students’ understanding of how the Qur’an was revealed, preserved, compiled, used as a source of law, and regarded as the foundation of Islamic belief and practice.

Revelation

Revelation of the Qur’an

Students study the revelation of the Qur’an to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ between 610 and 632, including the first revelation, gradual revelation, and the role of Angel Jibril عليه السلام.

Compilation

Compilation of the Qur’an

Students learn how the Qur’an was preserved and compiled under the Rightly Guided Caliphs, especially during the periods of Abu Bakr رضي الله عنه and Uthman رضي الله عنه.

Themes

Major Themes of the Qur’an

Students connect the prescribed passages with wider Qur’anic teachings, especially belief in Allah, guidance, accountability, worship, prophethood, and moral conduct.

Law

Qur’an in Legal Thinking

Students understand how the Qur’an functions as the primary source of Islamic law and guidance for Muslim belief, worship, morality, and social life.

Sources

Relationship with Other Sources

Students study the Qur’an’s relationship with Hadiths, consensus / ijma‘, and analogy / qiyas in Islamic legal thinking.

Significance

Basis of Thought and Action

Students learn why the Qur’an is central to Muslim belief, worship, conduct, law, personal life, and community life.

3. The Life and Importance of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

This section covers the main events of the Prophet’s ﷺ life, his Makkan and Madinan mission, his leadership, his character, and his significance as the final Messenger of Allah.

Early Life

Birth to Call to Prophethood

Students study the main events from the Prophet’s ﷺ birth to his call to prophethood, including his family background, early character, and preparation for prophethood.

Makka

Activities in Makka

Students learn about early preaching, opposition from the Quraysh, persecution, patience, migration-related events, and the Prophet’s ﷺ experiences with opponents.

Madina

Activities in Madina

Students study migration, community building, leadership in Madina, relations with different groups, conflicts with the Makkans, and the development of the Muslim community.

Character

Actions and Character

Students explore the Prophet’s ﷺ honesty, mercy, patience, justice, humility, forgiveness, courage, trust in Allah, and care for others.

Example

Model for Muslim Individuals

Students understand how the Prophet’s ﷺ actions provide examples for personal conduct, family life, social relations, and dealings with women and non-Muslims.

Final Messenger

Seal of the Prophets

Students learn the significance of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ as the Seal of the Prophets and the last Messenger of God.

4. The First Islamic Community

This section introduces the key people who lived with and near the Prophet ﷺ, including his wives, descendants, leading Companions, scribes, Emigrants, Helpers, and the four Rightly Guided Caliphs during his lifetime.

Family

The Prophet’s Wives

Students study the names, roles, virtues, and significance of the Mothers of the Believers in the early Muslim community.

Family

Descendants of the Prophet ﷺ

Students learn about the Prophet’s ﷺ children, grandchildren, and the direct line recognised among Shi‘a Muslims as Imams.

Companions

Leading Companions

Students study the major Companions who supported the Prophet ﷺ, including the Ten Blessed Companions and important figures around him.

Scribes

The Prophet’s Scribes

Students learn about those who wrote revelation and correspondence, and their importance in preserving and communicating the message of Islam.

Community

Emigrants and Helpers

Students study the Muhajirun and Ansar, their sacrifices, brotherhood, support, and role in building the early Muslim community.

Leadership

Four Rightly Guided Caliphs During the Prophet’s ﷺ Lifetime

Students study Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, and Ali رضي الله عنهم during the Prophet’s ﷺ lifetime, before their later rules as Caliphs.

How Paper 1 Will Be Taught

The aim is not only to complete the syllabus, but to train students to write accurate, relevant, and well-developed exam answers.

  • Topic-wise explanation of the full syllabus
  • Arabic and English understanding of selected passages
  • Key facts, names, events, dates, and Islamic terms
  • 10-mark descriptive answer practice
  • 4-mark significance and evaluation practice
  • Past paper question analysis
  • Common mistakes and examiner-style improvement

Why This Outline Is Useful

Many students study Islamiyat as scattered information. This outline arranges Paper 1 in the same order as the syllabus so that each topic can later be opened as a separate notes page.
