Cambridge + examiner-report strategy

Paper 2 focus:
Cambridge sets major Hadith teachings for close study. Students should know what Muslims should believe and how they should act.
Part A:
Give accurate, relevant, detailed knowledge. Do not merely paraphrase; explain what the Prophet ﷺ is instructing Muslims to do.
Part B:
Give developed reasoning, present-day application and judgement. Two well-developed reasons are usually stronger than many weak points.
A* move:
Use supporting Hadith references, Qur’anic links where suitable, practical examples, and a final judgement sentence.

A* Answer Blueprint

Read-only revision
Best Hadiths to revise: H1 · Religion is sincerityH2 · Want for your brother what you want for yourselfH3 · Speak good or keep silent; honour neighbour and guestH4 · Obligatory prayers, Ramadan fast, lawful and unlawfulH5 · Every joint owes charity: justice, help, good word, prayer steps, removing harmH6 · Change evil by hand, tongue, or heartH9 · Best food is from one’s own labourH10 · Caring for widow and poor equals striving/worshipH11 · Guardian of orphan close to Prophet ﷺ in ParadiseH12 · Be gentle; do not be hard; give glad tidings; do not alienateH13 · Qur’an is like tethered camels: revise it or it escapesH14 · Allah has mercy on kindness in buying, selling, demanding repayment
Part A maximum content plan
  1. Decode the question first: Everyday life means routine choices: speech, earning, worship, family, social media, neighbours and business.
  2. Use examples from morning to night: intention, prayer, work, speech, helping others, buying/selling, revising Qur’an.
  3. Select Hadiths across individual and communal categories so the answer feels complete.
  4. Start with a definition: Hadiths record the Prophet’s ﷺ sayings, actions and approvals; Sunnah is his practical way of living Islam.
  5. Explain that Hadiths translate belief into action: they show Muslims how to live the Qur’an in real situations.
  6. Use Qur’anic link 33:21: the Prophet ﷺ is the best example; therefore his Hadiths guide worship, manners, family life, community life and law.
  7. Use Qur’anic link 4:59 or 59:7 to show that obedience to the Messenger ﷺ is part of obedience to Allah.
  8. For personal guidance: use Hadith 1, 3, 4, 9, 17, 18, 19 and 20 to cover sincerity, speech, worship, halal earning, modesty, humility, self-control and inner faith.
  9. For communal guidance: use Hadith 2, 5, 10, 11, 12, 14, 15 and 16 to cover brotherhood, charity, vulnerable people, gentle leadership, business mercy and unity.
  10. For moral reform: use Hadith 6 to explain changing evil according to ability and wisdom.
  11. For knowledge and worship: use Hadith 13 to show that Qur’anic learning needs revision and discipline.
  12. Explain that Hadiths guide both ordinary actions and major moral choices: speaking, earning, buying, selling, helping, forgiving, teaching and correcting wrong.
  13. Show that Hadiths link belief and action: faith is not only what one claims but what appears in heart, speech, deeds and treatment of others.
  14. Conclude that Hadiths remain relevant because human problems — greed, pride, injustice, loneliness, conflict and weak worship — still need prophetic guidance.
Part B maximum evaluation plan
  1. Take a balanced judgement: the teachings are clear and practical, but acting on them needs discipline against ego, social pressure and habits.
  2. Balanced answer: Hadiths are easy because they are practical; difficult because ego and society resist them.
  3. If asked why the Prophet’s ﷺ example matters: he is the chosen Messenger who demonstrated Allah’s guidance practically; Muslims need a living model, not only rules.
  4. If asked how obeying the Prophet ﷺ is obeying Allah: Allah commanded obedience to His Messenger; following Sunnah is a response to divine command.
  5. If asked why belief and action both matter: belief gives intention and accountability, while action proves sincerity and benefits society.
  6. If asked whether Hadiths are easy to act upon: give a balanced view — simple teachings are clear, but ego, social pressure, materialism and online culture make practice challenging.
  7. Give two fully developed reasons rather than many shallow points: e.g., speech control reduces harm; mercy to vulnerable people brings reward and social welfare.
  8. Use practical examples: stopping gossip, helping an orphan, earning honestly, dealing gently in trade, respecting neighbours, revising Qur’an, avoiding pride.
  9. A* evaluation move: explain not only what Muslims should do, but why it matters spiritually and socially.
  10. End with a judgement sentence: Hadiths are practical, but their benefit depends on sincerity, knowledge and consistent application.
Scholarship / source links to remember
  • Cambridge syllabus: Hadith passages are studied for belief and action, especially individual conduct and life in the community.
  • Use the set Hadiths recorded in Sahih al-Bukhari and/or Sahih Muslim, as Cambridge states for Appendix 2.
  • Helpful Qur’anic links: 33:21 for the Prophet ﷺ as model; 4:59 and 59:7 for obedience to the Messenger ﷺ; 49:10 for brotherhood; 5:2 for cooperation in good.
  • Use terms carefully: ikhlas, sunnah, taqwa, sadaqah, haya, halal, haram, ummah, mercy, accountability.
Common mistakes to avoid
  • Do not turn a guidance question into a history of Hadith compilation.
  • Do not say Hadiths replace the Qur’an; they explain and practically demonstrate Qur’anic guidance.
  • Do not give Part B as a one-line opinion; it must have reasons, examples and judgement.
Writing frame: Opening sentence → 4–6 developed teaching paragraphs for Part A → short concluding link to the question. For Part B, write two developed evaluation paragraphs with “because / therefore / for example”, then a final judgement sentence.

In your opinion, why is brotherhood given so much importance in Islam? Give reasons to support your answer. [4]

A* Answer Blueprint

Use this as a planning map. Select the most relevant points; do not write every bullet if time is short.

Read-only revision
Best Hadiths to revise: H1 · Religion is sincerityH2 · Want for your brother what you want for yourselfH3 · Speak good or keep silent; honour neighbour and guestH5 · Every joint owes charity: justice, help, good word, prayer steps, removing harmH6 · Change evil by hand, tongue, or heartH10 · Caring for widow and poor equals striving/worshipH11 · Guardian of orphan close to Prophet ﷺ in ParadiseH12 · Be gentle; do not be hard; give glad tidings; do not alienateH15 · Allah will not show mercy to one who does not show mercy to othersH16 · Believers are like a single body
Part A maximum content plan
  1. Decode the question first: Brotherhood must be shown as a practical system, not only emotion.
  2. Use Hadith 2 and 16 as your backbone; support them with Hadiths on speech, charity, widows/orphans and mercy.
  3. Explain how each Hadith removes one cause of division: selfishness, gossip, neglect, harshness or arrogance.
  4. Open by explaining that Islamic brotherhood is not casual friendship; it is a faith-based bond built on shared belief, mercy, rights and responsibility.
  5. Use Hadith 2 as a central proof: wanting for one’s brother what one wants for oneself creates empathy, equality and protection from envy.
  6. Use Hadith 16 as the strongest image of unity: believers are like one body; pain in one part affects the whole, so Muslims should not be indifferent to each other’s suffering.
  7. Use Hadith 1 to explain sincerity to common Muslims and leaders; unity requires sincere advice, loyalty to truth and avoidance of betrayal.
  8. Use Hadith 3 to show unity through speech: good words and silence from harmful speech prevent quarrels and slander.
  9. Use Hadith 5 to show practical brotherhood: justice, help, good words and removing harm create daily solidarity.
  10. Use Hadith 10 and 11 to show that brotherhood includes the weak: widows, poor people and orphans are not left alone.
  11. Use Hadith 12 to show that gentleness keeps people connected; harshness divides communities.
  12. Use Hadith 15 to show mercy as the emotional foundation of brotherhood.
  13. Use Hadith 6 carefully: correcting evil can protect unity when done wisely, but harshness or arrogance can damage it.
  14. Conclude by saying the set Hadiths build brotherhood through inner sincerity, good speech, shared pain, social service and mercy.
Part B maximum evaluation plan
  1. Core argument: brotherhood is central because Islam builds an ummah, not isolated worshippers.
  2. Stress that brotherhood protects unity of the ummah and makes Muslim society caring rather than competitive.
  3. Reason 1: Islam gives brotherhood importance because Muslims are one ummah and should not live as isolated individuals.
  4. Reason 2: brotherhood protects the weak and ensures that the poor, orphaned, widowed and oppressed are not abandoned.
  5. Reason 3: unity prevents internal conflict, sectarian hatred, racism, family feuds and social fragmentation.
  6. Reason 4: a united community is stronger in worship, education, charity, da‘wah and moral reform.
  7. Reason 5: brotherhood reflects the Prophet’s ﷺ model in Madinah, where Muslims built support across tribal and social divisions.
  8. A* evaluation move: mention that unity does not mean everyone thinks exactly the same; it means disagreement is handled with mercy, justice and respect.
  9. Modern link: online abuse, sectarian labelling and social comparison weaken brotherhood; Hadith teachings offer a corrective.

  • Helpful Qur’anic links: 33:21 for the Prophet ﷺ as model; 4:59 and 59:7 for obedience to the Messenger ﷺ; 49:10 for brotherhood; 5:2 for cooperation in good.
  • Use terms carefully: ikhlas, sunnah, taqwa, sadaqah, haya, halal, haram, ummah, mercy, accountability.
Common mistakes to avoid
  • Do not reduce communal conduct to charity only; include speech, neighbour rights, mercy, business ethics, unity and leadership.
  • Do not confuse sadaqah with obligatory zakat when using Hadith 5.
  • Do not give modern examples without linking them back to a Hadith teaching.
Writing frame: Opening sentence → 4–6 developed teaching paragraphs for Part A → short concluding link to the question. For Part B, write two developed evaluation paragraphs with “because / therefore / for example”, then a final judgement sentence.
