Examiner-report intelligence added to this page

  • Read the exact command carefully: do not turn a specific question into a general prepared answer.
  • For Hadith teaching questions, do not only paraphrase the translation; explain the wider teaching and its effect on Muslim life.
  • Separate Part A and Part B clearly: Part A usually needs knowledge/teaching; Part B needs application, judgement, and developed reasons.
  • Use practical examples from the Prophet ﷺ, the Companions, or modern Muslim life to push evaluation answers into a higher level.

High-scoring preparation guide

Exam move

Exam move

Individual Conduct

Personal morality, faith in action, sincerity, self-control, lawful conduct, and daily Muslim behaviour.

2 question pairs20222019
1Using four of the set Hadiths, outline the Prophet’s teachings about how a Muslim should conduct their personal life. [1…Oct-Nov V-2 • 2022
A
Using four of the set Hadiths, outline the Prophet’s teachings about how a Muslim should conduct their personal life. [10]
B
In your opinion, what are the communal benefits of following the Prophet’s Hadiths? Give reasons to support your answer. [4]

Tips to prepare this answer

Part A: content plan
  • Choose four set Hadiths before writing; do not write all 20 randomly. A strong combination may cover sincerity, lawful conduct, control of speech, modesty, charity/removing harm, and brotherhood.
  • For each Hadith use a mini-paragraph: name/short reference of the Hadith → main teaching → how it shapes personal behaviour.
  • Keep the focus on personal life: intention, worship, honesty, earning halal, avoiding harm, controlling anger/tongue, modesty, and accountability.
Part B: evaluation plan
  • Move from individual reform to communal benefit: sincere people create trust; controlled speech reduces conflict; lawful conduct reduces corruption; charity builds care.
  • Give two developed reasons rather than a list. Link each reason to real community life: family, school, mosque, online behaviour, neighbours, or business dealings.
Avoid this mistake
  • Do not only quote or paraphrase four Hadiths; Cambridge rewards explanation of teaching.
  • Do not turn Part B into another list of Hadith teachings; it asks for communal benefits.

2From the set Hadiths you have studied outline the Prophet’s teachings on the individual conduct of Muslims. [10]Oct-Nov V-1 • 2019
A
From the set Hadiths you have studied outline the Prophet’s teachings on the individual conduct of Muslims. [10]
B
How do Muslims benefit in their community life by following the guidance given in the Prophet’s Hadiths? [4]

Tips to prepare this answer

Part A: content plan
  • Group your answer under qualities: sincerity, moral discipline, lawful behaviour, humility, good speech, service, and brotherhood.
  • Use at least four set Hadiths and make each Hadith do real work: what it teaches, what action it demands, and what kind of Muslim character it creates.
  • Add precise terms such as ikhlas, taqwa, halal/haram, haya, accountability, and Sunnah when relevant.
Part B: evaluation plan
  • Explain how personal discipline becomes public benefit: fewer lies, less backbiting, more neighbourly support, greater trust in trade and family life.
  • Use one modern example, such as social media speech, helping vulnerable people, respecting workers, or avoiding dishonest earning.
Avoid this mistake
  • Do not write a general essay on “good character” without linking points to set Hadiths.
  • Do not ignore the word “individual”; community benefits belong mainly in Part B.

Communal Conduct

Care, service, social responsibility, helping others, mosque/community life, and social improvement.

2 question pairs20162014
1Giving references from the set Hadiths you have studied, outline the Prophet’s teachings about care in the community. [1…Oct-Nov V-2 • 2016
A
Giving references from the set Hadiths you have studied, outline the Prophet’s teachings about care in the community. [10]
B
How can the teachings of the Prophet concerning care in the community be applied today? [4]

Tips to prepare this answer

Part A: content plan
  • Select Hadiths that clearly show care: loving for one’s brother what one loves for oneself, honouring neighbour and guest, speaking good or staying silent, removing harm from the road, and believers being like one body.
  • For each reference, explain the specific form of care: emotional support, protection from harm, service, respectful speech, generosity, and shared responsibility.
  • Build the answer from simple care to wider social welfare: home → neighbours → mosque/community → whole ummah.
Part B: evaluation plan
  • Give practical applications: food banks, visiting the sick, helping refugees/orphans, avoiding online slander, neighbourhood support, and resolving disputes.
  • Show why application matters today: loneliness, poverty, family breakdown, sectarianism, and digital harm can be reduced through prophetic conduct.
Avoid this mistake
  • Do not give vague advice such as “be good to people” without Hadith-based examples.
  • Do not discuss only worship rituals; this question is about care in community life.

A
Outline the main teachings of the set Hadiths about the conduct of Muslims in communal life. [10]
B
How do you think following the guidance of the Prophet ﷺ as regards communal living can improve society today? [4]

Tips to prepare this answer

Part A: content plan
  • Organise around communal values: brotherhood, neighbourly duty, responsible speech, mutual mercy, justice, and removing harm.
  • Use set Hadiths as evidence and explain their community effect: protection of dignity, peace, cooperation, social equality, and compassion.
  • Include the Prophet’s ﷺ model as practical Sunnah, not only as a historical reference.
Part B: evaluation plan
  • Choose two ways society improves: less conflict through good speech, stronger welfare through charity and service, or greater unity through brotherhood.
  • Develop each point with “because” and “therefore”: because people protect one another’s rights, society becomes safer and more trusting.
Avoid this mistake
  • Do not repeat Part A word-for-word. Part B needs judgement on improvement today.
  • Do not make modern examples too casual; keep them linked to Islamic values.

Unity and Brotherhood

Brotherhood, unity of the ummah, mutual care, social harmony, and the Prophet’s ﷺ example as a living model.

3 question pairs202320212017
1From the teachings given in the set Hadiths, write an account of how they help establish brotherhood in the community. […May-June V-1 • 2023
A
From the teachings given in the set Hadiths, write an account of how they help establish brotherhood in the community. [10]
B
In your opinion, why is brotherhood given so much importance in Islam? Give reasons to support your answer. [4]

Tips to prepare this answer

Part A: content plan
  • Build the answer around brotherhood: love for others, mercy, shared pain, avoiding harm, helping the weak, honouring neighbours, and sincere advice.
  • Use Hadiths that naturally support brotherhood; do not force unrelated Hadiths. The “one body” image is very useful because it shows emotional and practical unity.
  • Explain how each Hadith changes behaviour: it prevents selfishness, envy, racism, backbiting, neglect, and social division.
Part B: evaluation plan
  • Argue that brotherhood protects the ummah from division and makes faith visible in action.
  • Give a reason from daily life: people cooperate in hardship, support the poor, defend each other’s dignity, and resolve disputes before they grow.
Avoid this mistake
  • Do not write only about friendship; Islamic brotherhood is a religious and social duty.
  • Do not list benefits without explaining why Islam gives brotherhood such importance.

Referring to the set Hadiths write about the benefits to the community of following the Prophet’s guidance. [10]

Why is the example of the Prophet ﷺ important for Muslims today? [4]

Tips to prepare this answer

Part A: content plan
  • Write about benefits, not only teachings: guidance from the Prophet ﷺ produces trust, mercy, discipline, respect for rights, and social stability.
  • Use different types of benefit: moral benefit, family benefit, community benefit, legal/social benefit, and spiritual benefit.
  • Refer to set Hadiths briefly and then explain the community outcome. A developed explanation earns more than a long list.
Part B: evaluation plan
  • Explain why the Prophet’s ﷺ example matters: he is the practical model of the Qur’an and shows how faith is lived in real situations.

Avoid this mistake
  • Do not make the answer only theoretical. The word “benefits” needs visible results in community life.
  • Do not ignore the Prophet ﷺ in Part B; the question is specifically about his example.

A
Write an account of how the Hadiths of the Prophet stress upon the unity of the Islamic community. Use at least four set Hadiths from the syllabus to develop your answer. [10]
B
Why do you think Islam gives so much importance to maintaining unity within the Islamic community? [4]

Tips to prepare this answer

Part A: content plan
  • The question demands at least four set Hadiths, so visibly use four. Numbering them in your plan before writing helps avoid missing the requirement.
  • Choose unity-focused Hadiths: believers like one body, love for brother, honour neighbour/guest, speak good or remain silent, remove harm, and sincerity to the community/leaders.
  • After each Hadith, explain exactly how unity is created: shared concern, fewer insults, mutual service, stronger trust, and protection of rights.
Part B: evaluation plan
  • Explain that unity is important because Muslims form one ummah; disunity weakens worship, social justice, and public strength.
  • Use contemporary examples carefully: sectarian conflict, family disputes, online abuse, or community rivalry can be reduced through these teachings.
Avoid this mistake
  • Do not use fewer than four Hadiths in Part A.
  • Do not confuse unity with uniformity; Muslims may differ but must preserve respect, mercy, and common faith.

Hadith as a Source of Guidance

Hadith as practical guidance, relationship of belief and action, obeying the Prophet ﷺ, and living faith in everyday life.

4 question pairs2023202120162015
1From the teachings given in the set Hadiths, write an account of the guidance given to Muslims in their everyday life. […Oct-Nov V-1 • 2023
A
From the teachings given in the set Hadiths, write an account of the guidance given to Muslims in their everyday life. [10]
B
In your opinion, how easy are the Hadiths to act upon? Give reasons to support your answer. [4]

Tips to prepare this answer

Part A: content plan
  • Use at least three set Hadiths to reach a strong level, and four if possible. Identify each Hadith clearly before explaining it.
  • Cover everyday guidance in both personal and communal life: intention, worship, halal conduct, speech, neighbours, brotherhood, charity, humility, and accountability.
  • For each Hadith, write: teaching → daily action → benefit in this world and the next.
Part B: evaluation plan
  • Take a clear view: easy, difficult, or balanced. Then support it with reasons. Easy because the teachings are simple and practical; difficult because ego, society, social media, and materialism test Muslims.
  • Use examples of how a Muslim can still act upon them gradually: controlling speech, helping neighbours, avoiding gossip, and making sincere repentance.
Avoid this mistake
  • Do not write only benefits in Part B; the question asks how easy they are to act upon.
  • Do not give a prepared general answer; match the wording “everyday life” and “how easy”.

2Write about the importance of knowing the Prophet’s Hadiths and following his Sunna for Muslims. [10]May-June V-2 • 2021
A
Write about the importance of knowing the Prophet’s Hadiths and following his Sunna for Muslims. [10]
B
Why are belief and action both important for Muslims? Give reasons to support your answer. [4]

Tips to prepare this answer

Part A: content plan
  • Start by defining Hadith and Sunnah briefly: Hadith records the Prophet’s ﷺ sayings/actions/approvals; Sunnah is his practical way.
  • Explain why Muslims need them: they explain Qur’anic commands, show worship in practice, guide character, support law, and provide a model for family and community life.
  • Use examples: prayer details, zakat practice, halal/haram conduct, neighbour rights, brotherhood, and sincerity.
Part B: evaluation plan
  • Show that belief without action is incomplete, while action without sincere belief becomes empty.
  • Develop two reasons: actions prove faith in real life; belief gives intention and accountability behind actions.
Avoid this mistake
  • Do not discuss only the history of Hadith compilation; this is about importance and following Sunnah.
  • Do not make Part B a slogan; explain the relationship between iman and amal.

3What is the role of Hadiths as a source of guidance in the lives of Muslims? [10]May-June V-1 • 2016
A
What is the role of Hadiths as a source of guidance in the lives of Muslims? [10]
B
How are Muslims obeying God when they obey the Prophet? [4]

Tips to prepare this answer

Part A: content plan
  • Explain Hadiths as practical guidance: they show how Qur’anic principles are lived by the Prophet ﷺ in worship, morals, family, community, and law.
  • Use clear examples: how to pray, speak truthfully, avoid harm, honour guests/neighbours, love fellow Muslims, earn honestly, and act with sincerity.
  • Balance belief and practice: Hadiths guide both inner attitude and outward behaviour.
Part B: evaluation plan
  • Use Qur’anic reasoning in your own words: Muslims obey Allah by following the Messenger because Allah chose him to teach and demonstrate His guidance.
  • Give one practical example: following the Prophet’s ﷺ method of prayer or moral conduct is obedience to God’s command, not merely respect for a historical figure.
Avoid this mistake
  • Do not answer as if Hadiths replace the Qur’an; show that they work with and explain the Qur’an.
  • Do not leave Part B as one sentence; develop the link between obedience to Prophet ﷺ and obedience to Allah.

A
Give an account of how the Prophet’s Hadiths have been a source of guidance to Muslims in putting their faith into practice. [10]
B
Why do the Prophet’s Hadiths link belief and action so closely? [4]

Tips to prepare this answer

Part A: content plan
  • Focus on “putting faith into practice”: choose Hadiths that translate belief into action—sincerity, prayer, charity, speech, brotherhood, lawful conduct, and care for others.
  • For each point, show the movement from faith to action: belief in accountability leads to good speech; belief in Allah leads to sincerity; belief in brotherhood leads to helping others.
  • Use examples from everyday Muslim life, not only abstract moral words.
Part B: evaluation plan
  • Explain that Hadiths link belief and action because Islam is not only inner conviction; it must appear in conduct, worship, and treatment of others.
  • Use two developed reasons: actions show sincerity of faith; actions protect society from harm and make Islamic values visible.
Avoid this mistake
  • Do not write only about the authenticity or compilation of Hadiths; this question asks about guidance.
  • Do not separate belief and action as if they are unrelated; the whole answer should show their connection.
