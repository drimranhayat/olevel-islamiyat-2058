Past Paper Practice Programme

Past papers are not only for revision. They teach students how Cambridge-style questions are worded, how marks are earned, and how answers should be organised.

Topic-Wise

Practice by Syllabus Topic

Students practise questions from Qur’an, Hadith, Seerah, Caliphs, beliefs and practices separately so they can improve weak areas first.

  • Qur’anic passages
  • Seerah and early community
  • Hadith and Caliphs
  • Faith and practices
Answer Skills

10-Mark and 4-Mark Answers

Students learn the difference between descriptive knowledge answers and significance-based evaluation answers.

  • Paragraph structure
  • Relevant detail
  • Clear explanation
  • Evaluation and reasoning
Exam Strategy

Timing and Question Choice

Students practise how to manage time, understand question command words, and choose questions wisely during the exam.

  • Time management
  • Question reading
  • Planning before writing
  • Avoiding irrelevant detail

Why Past Paper Practice Matters

Many students study the full syllabus but still lose marks because they do not understand how to write according to the question. Past paper practice solves this problem.

  • Students understand repeated question patterns
  • Students learn how much detail is needed
  • Students improve answer organisation
  • Students become confident under timed conditions
  • Students learn how to avoid vague or general answers

How Practice Sessions Work

Each session begins with question understanding, then planning, answer development, correction and improvement.

From Question to Model Answer

Students are shown how to break a question into parts, select relevant material, organise the response and add significance where required.

Past Paper Areas Covered

Practice sessions can be arranged for complete papers or selected weak areas depending on the student's preparation level.

Paper 1

Qur’anic Themes

Practice includes passage-based questions, theme explanation and relevance for Muslims today.

Paper 1

Qur’an History and Seerah

Students practise revelation, compilation, Makkan life, Madinan life and major events from the Prophet’s ﷺ life.

Paper 1

First Islamic Community

Practice covers wives, descendants, Companions, Emigrants, Helpers, scribes and early Muslim community figures.

Paper 2

Hadith Teachings

Students practise selected Hadith explanation and application to individual and community life.

Paper 2

Caliphs and Leadership

Practice covers events, policies, achievements, difficulties and leadership lessons of the Rightly Guided Caliphs.

Paper 2

Faith and Practices

Students practise questions on Articles of Faith, Five Pillars and the wider meanings of Jihad.
