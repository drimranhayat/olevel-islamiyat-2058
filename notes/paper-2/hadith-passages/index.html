Cambridge Alignment

What Paper 2 expects from these Hadiths

Cambridge sets these passages under “Major teachings in the Hadiths of the Prophet.” Students should study teachings in each passage about what Muslims should believe and how they should act.

Group 1

Individual Conduct

Personal sincerity, worship, morality, lawful earning, Qur’an, modesty and inner purity.

Group 2

Community Life

Brotherhood, speech, neighbours, guests, charity, welfare, mercy, trade and unity.

Exam Skill

Arabic + English

Passages are set in Arabic and English, so students should recognise both.

A* Method

Belief + Action

Always explain both what the Hadith teaches Muslims to believe and how it changes behaviour.

Answer Method

5-step method for any Hadith passage

This simple structure prevents vague answers and keeps students close to the Cambridge task.

1

Recognise

Identify the Hadith by number, key words and theme.

2

Explain

State the teaching in your own words without inventing extra details.

3

Belief

Show what Muslims learn about faith, Allah, the Hereafter or accountability.

4

Action

Explain how Muslims should act in worship, character or society.

5

Importance

End with why the teaching matters for Muslims today.

Memory System

How students should memorise the Hadith passages

⌄

Do this

  • Memorise the number, key Arabic phrase and English meaning.
  • Group passages by theme rather than memorising randomly.
  • For each Hadith, learn one belief point and one action point.
  • Practise writing a short teaching paragraph and a short application paragraph.
  • Use exact Cambridge passage wording when answering exam questions.

Avoid this

  • Do not invent long stories around the Hadith.
  • Do not quote uncertain wording as if it is exact.
  • Do not write only a translation with no explanation.
  • Do not ignore Arabic recognition.
  • Do not confuse individual conduct with community life when classifying themes.
