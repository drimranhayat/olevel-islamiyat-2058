O Level Islamiyat Paper 2 Complete Syllabus Outline

A clear, syllabus-based roadmap for Cambridge O Level Islamiyat 2058 Paper 2, covering Hadith teachings, the history and importance of Hadiths, the Rightly Guided Caliphs, Articles of Faith, Jihad, and the Pillars of Islam.

Paper 2 at a Glance

  • Written paper: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Total marks: 50
  • Question 1 and Question 2 are compulsory
  • Students answer two further questions
  • Focus on Hadith, leadership, belief, practice and significance
  • Ideal for topic-wise notes and answer-writing practice

Paper 2 Syllabus Areas

Paper 2 is built around four major syllabus areas. Each area requires accurate knowledge, clear explanation, and the ability to discuss the significance of Hadith teachings, leadership, beliefs and practices in Muslim life.

Section 1

Major Teachings in the Hadiths

Close study of selected Hadith passages, their teachings, and their importance for individual conduct and community life.

Section 2

History & Importance of the Hadiths

Compilation, preservation, authentication, collections, compilers, legal significance, and the role of Hadiths in Islamic thought and action.

Section 3

Rightly Guided Caliphs

Main events, policies, achievements, difficulties, leadership qualities, and importance of the four Caliphs as models for later Muslims.

Section 4

Articles of Faith & Pillars of Islam

The Six Articles of Faith, Jihad in its range of meanings, and the Five Pillars of Islam with their method and significance.

1. Major Teachings in the Hadiths of the Prophet ﷺ

Students study selected Hadiths in Arabic and English. The focus is on understanding what Muslims should believe, how Muslims should act, and how Hadith teachings guide personal conduct and community life.

Hadith Group 1

Individual Conduct

This group focuses on personal faith, sincerity, speech, charity, moral responsibility, lawful conduct, work, modesty, and self-discipline.

  • Sincerity in religion
  • Love for others
  • Speaking good or remaining silent
  • Obligatory duties
  • Charity and removing harm
Hadith Group 2

Life in the Community

This group explores social responsibility, helping the poor, caring for orphans, gentleness in leadership, mercy, unity, and concern for others.

  • Care for widows and the poor
  • Care for orphans
  • Gentleness and encouragement
  • Mercy towards others
  • Believers as one body
Special Study

Hadiths for Special Study

Students prepare the prescribed Hadith passages with their meaning, message, Arabic recognition, English explanation, and relevance to Muslim life.

  • Hadith 1 to Hadith 20
  • Meaning and message
  • Application in daily life
  • Part (a) and part (b) practice

2. The History and Importance of the Hadiths

This section develops students’ understanding of how Hadiths were preserved, collected, authenticated, organised, and used as a major source of Islamic guidance and legal thinking.

Compilation

History of Hadith Compilation

Students study how Hadiths were transmitted, preserved, written, collected, and compiled by early Muslims.

Early Collections

Earliest Hadith Collections

Students learn about early efforts to collect and preserve Hadith material before the major later compilations.

Collections

Musnad and Musannaf Collections

Students understand the difference between musnad and musannaf arrangements and why Hadith collections were organised in different ways.

Compilers

Main Compilers and Their Activities

Students study the work of major Hadith compilers, their journeys, methods, selection criteria, and contribution to preserving Hadiths.

Authentication

Isnad and Matn

Students learn how scholars examined the chain of transmitters and the text of a Hadith to test its reliability.

Collections

Sunni and Shi‘a Hadith Collections

Students study the main features of the six collections of Sunni Hadiths and the four collections of Shi‘a Hadiths.

Themes

Major Themes of the Hadiths

Students connect the prescribed Hadiths with wider themes such as worship, sincerity, justice, mercy, charity, leadership, and community responsibility.

Law

Hadiths in Legal Thinking

Students understand how Hadiths function as a source of guidance in Islamic law and how they relate to the Qur’an, ijma‘ and qiyas.

Significance

Hadiths in Thought and Action

Students learn why Hadiths are important for belief, worship, law, morality, family life, community life, and the daily conduct of Muslims.

3. The Period of Rule of the Rightly Guided Caliphs

This section covers the main events of the rules of Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman and Ali رضي الله عنهم, their policies, achievements, difficulties, leadership styles, and importance as models for Muslim government and community life.

Events

Main Events of Their Rules

Students study the major events that took place during the rule of each Caliph and their effects on the early Muslim community.

State

Maintaining and Expanding the State

Students learn how the Caliphs maintained unity, dealt with crises, expanded the state, and organised administration.

Leadership

Approaches to Leadership

Students explore justice, consultation, courage, simplicity, accountability, piety, and service as leadership qualities.

Achievements

Main Achievements

Students study the key achievements of each Caliph in government, military affairs, administration, preservation of Islam, and community leadership.

Difficulties

Main Difficulties Encountered

Students understand the challenges faced by the Caliphs, including rebellion, expansion pressures, internal disputes, and political conflicts.

Model

Importance as Models for Later Muslims

Students learn how the Caliphs provide examples of leadership, government, justice, consultation, public service, and relations with other states.

4. Articles of Faith, Jihad and Pillars of Islam

This section covers the main beliefs and practices of Islam. Students should know what each belief or practice means and understand its significance in the faith and action of individual Muslims and Muslim communities.

Beliefs

Six Articles of Faith

Students study the core beliefs of Islam and their significance for Muslim thought, worship, accountability, and daily conduct.

  • God
  • Angels
  • Books
  • Prophets
  • Predestination and decree
  • Resurrection and the Last Day
Jihad

Jihad in Its Range of Meanings

Students study Jihad carefully in its physical, mental, and spiritual meanings, with balanced and exam-appropriate explanation.

  • Spiritual struggle
  • Moral and mental effort
  • Physical struggle where relevant
  • Self-discipline and service
Practices

Five Pillars of Islam

Students study the main observances of Islam, how they are performed, and why they are important for individuals and the Muslim community.

  • Shahada
  • Salat
  • Zakat
  • Sawm
  • Hajj

How Paper 2 Will Be Taught

The aim is not only to complete the syllabus, but to train students to write accurate, relevant and well-developed exam answers.

  • Topic-wise explanation of the full syllabus
  • Arabic and English understanding of selected Hadiths
  • Key facts, names, terms, collections and Islamic concepts
  • 10-mark descriptive answer practice
  • 4-mark significance and evaluation practice
  • Past paper question analysis
  • Common mistakes and examiner-style improvement
